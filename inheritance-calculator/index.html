Understanding Islamic Inheritance (Mirath / Faraid)

Islamic inheritance follows precise rules from the Quran and Sunnah. This updated Sharia law inheritance calculator implements correct fixed shares, residuary distribution, and proportional reduction ('Awl) when shares exceed the estate, fully compliant with the Islamic law of inheritance.

Key Principles of Islamic Inheritance

  • Fixed shares are specified in the Quran (Surah An-Nisa)
  • Sons receive double the share of daughters
  • Debts and funeral expenses are paid first
  • Specific conditions determine fixed share vs residuary

Understanding 'Awl (Adjustment) and Radd (Return)

'Awl (Adjustment): When total fixed shares exceed 1 (100% of estate), all shares are proportionally reduced.
Radd (Return): When total fixed shares are less than 1, the remainder returns proportionally to eligible heirs (excluding spouse).

Methodology & Sources

This calculator implements the Islamic laws of inheritance (Mirath) as prescribed in the Quran (Surah An-Nisa, Verses 11, 12, and 176) and the Sunnah of Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ).

Calculation Logic

  • Fixed Shares (Faraid) are distributed first.
  • Residue is distributed to 'Asabah (residuary heirs) after fixed shares.
  • 'Awl (Proportional Reduction) is applied if shares exceed the estate.
  • Radd (Return) is applied if shares are less than the estate (excluding spouse).
