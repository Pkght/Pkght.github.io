    Understanding the Nisab Threshold

    Nisab is the minimum amount of wealth a Muslim must possess for one lunar year before Zakat becomes obligatory. It is based on the market value of either gold or silver.

    • Gold Nisab: Equivalent to 87.48 grams of gold.
    • Silver Nisab: Equivalent to 612.36 grams of silver.

    Many scholars encourage using the silver standard today because it results in a lower threshold, enabling more people to qualify for Zakat and increasing support for those in need.

    Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

    Who can receive Zakat?

    The Quran lists eight eligible groups, including the poor, the needy, those in debt, new Muslims, and travelers in hardship.

    What assets are Zakatable?

    Cash, gold, silver, business inventory, investments, and rental income are subject to Zakat. Personal belongings such as your house, car, and clothing are not.

    When do I pay my Zakat?

    Zakat is due once a full lunar year passes on your wealth. Many people pay it during Ramadan for increased reward, but it may be paid at any time of the year.
