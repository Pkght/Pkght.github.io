What is Zakat?

Zakat, one of the five pillars of Islam, is an obligatory act of charity that requires Muslims who meet the necessary criteria of wealth to donate a certain portion of their wealth, typically 2.5%, to eligible recipients. It is a spiritual and social duty that purifies the wealth of the giver and provides for the needs of the poor and destitute.

Understanding the Nisab Threshold

The Nisab is the minimum amount of wealth a Muslim must own before they are obligated to pay Zakat. If your total net worth is below the Nisab threshold, you are not required to pay Zakat. The Nisab can be calculated based on the current market value of either gold or silver.

  • Gold Standard: The Nisab is the equivalent of 87.48 grams of gold.
  • Silver Standard: The Nisab is the equivalent of 612.36 grams of silver.

This calculator uses the silver standard, which is often recommended by scholars as it results in a lower threshold, thus allowing for a greater distribution of wealth to the needy.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Who is eligible to receive Zakat?

According to the Quran, Zakat can be distributed to eight categories of people: the poor, the needy, Zakat collectors, new converts to Islam, to free captives, those in debt, in the cause of Allah, and to stranded travelers.

What assets are subject to Zakat?

Zakat is payable on assets such as cash, gold, silver, investments, business inventory, and rental income. Personal items like your primary home, car, and clothing are generally not included.

When should I pay my Zakat?

Zakat is paid annually on the wealth that has been in your possession for a full lunar year. Many Muslims choose to pay their Zakat during the month of Ramadan to reap the multiplied rewards of giving charity in this blessed month.
